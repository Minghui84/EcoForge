Modern Steel Manufacturing & Innovations

Exploring Traditional Processes and Sustainable Innovations

1. Blast Furnace Process

Key Steps:

  • Iron ore, coke, and limestone are charged into the furnace
  • Hot air (1200°C) blasted into the bottom
  • Molten iron collected at bottom (1500°C)
  • Produces pig iron with 4-5% carbon content

2. Basic Oxygen Steelmaking

BOF Schematic

Characteristics:

  • Converts pig iron to steel by oxidizing impurities
  • Pure oxygen (99% purity) blown into molten metal
  • Process takes 15-20 minutes per batch
  • Produces steel with 0.1-1% carbon content

Hydrogen-Based Steel Production

Breakthrough Features:

  • Uses hydrogen instead of coke as reducing agent
  • Potential to reduce CO₂ emissions by 95%
  • Operates at lower temperatures (500-800°C)
  • Pilot plants operational in Sweden (HYBRIT)

Carbon Capture Utilization

Key Advantages:

  • Captures CO₂ emissions from blast furnace gases
  • Stores or repurposes CO₂ for chemical production
  • Can reduce plant emissions by 50-70%
  • Integrated with existing infrastructure
